# What is Strategic Planning?

---

## Introduction to Strategic Planning

Keywords:
- Essential process for organizational direction
- Defines priorities and actions for long-term success
- Provides structured approach to:
  - Clarifying mission and vision
  - Identifying key priorities
  - Setting measurable goals
  - Aligning resources
  - Tracking performance over time
Suggested Images:
- Group of people in a meeting
- Group of people discussing actively on a topic

<div class="notes">
Strategic planning is the essential process organizations use to define their direction, priorities, and actions to achieve long-term success. It provides a structured approach to clarifying mission and vision, identifying key priorities, setting measurable goals, aligning resources, and tracking performance over time. A strategic plan typically focuses on mid- to long-term goals and outlines the fundamental strategies required to attain them.
</div>

---

## Purpose of Strategic Planning

Keywords:
- Focus on mid- to long-term goals
- Provide clarity and accountability
- Create a roadmap for success
- Ensure daily operations contribute to long-term objectives
- Enable proactive approach to challenges
- Move beyond reactive management

Suggested Images:
- Map with specific route and destination
- Strategic road map with goals and action plans

<div class="notes">
The core purpose of strategic planning is to provide clarity, accountability, and a roadmap, ensuring that daily operations contribute to achieving long-term objectives and allowing leaders to proactively address challenges rather than merely reacting to them. This forward-thinking approach helps organizations stay focused and aligned with their ultimate goals.
</div>

## The Kernel of Good Strategy

Keywords:
- Strategy is a cohesive blend of:
  - Ideas
  - Analyses
  - Policies
  - Actions
- Response to important, high-stakes challenges
- Not to be confused with:
  - Ambition
  - Goals
  - Inspirational leadership

Suggested Images:
- Diagram of the Kernel of Good Strategy

<div class="notes">
According to Richard Rumelt, a strategy should be a cohesive blend of ideas, analyses, policies, and actions in response to an important, high-stakes challenge. He argues that many organizations confuse strategy with ambition, goals, or inspirational leadership. A good strategy centers on a core foundation, or kernel, composed of three essential components that we'll explore next.
</div>
