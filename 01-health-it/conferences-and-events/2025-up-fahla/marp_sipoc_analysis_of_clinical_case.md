# SIPOC Analysis

## Trauma Care Clinical Workflow

**Complete Analysis of Miguel Santos Case Scenario**
*Understanding Suppliers, Inputs, Process, Outputs, and Customers*

------

## 1. Accident Response and Transport

• **Suppliers:** Accident witnesses, Good Samaritans, community members, jeepney driver, patient's family, emergency hotline operators

• **Inputs:** Patient with traumatic injuries, accident scene assessment, patient identification, PhilHealth documents, transportation vehicle, basic first aid knowledge

• **Process:** Initial accident assessment, transport decision making, community mobilization, patient loading, continuous monitoring, family communication

• **Outputs:** Patient transported to hospital, basic vital status, preliminary injury assessment, family notification, accident documentation, ETA communication

• **Customers:** Patient requiring emergency care, hospital emergency department, family members, insurance providers

------

## 2. Hospital Registration Process

• **Suppliers:** Patient and family, PhilHealth system, hospital registration database, emergency department, medical records department

• **Inputs:** Patient demographics, insurance details, accident information, emergency contacts, medical history, identification documents

• **Process:** Data collection, MRN assignment, PhilHealth verification, insurance confirmation, contact documentation, wristband creation, medical record establishment

• **Outputs:** Completed registration record, medical record number, patient wristband, PhilHealth confirmation, ED logbook entry, billing account, patient chart

• **Customers:** ED clinical staff, patient, billing department, PhilHealth, quality assurance

------

## 3. Emergency Department Triage

• **Suppliers:** Registered patient, triage nurse, triage protocols, monitoring equipment, Philippine Triage Scale standards

• **Inputs:** Patient with trauma injuries, vital signs, injury mechanism, patient symptoms, clinical protocols, ED capacity status

• **Process:** Primary assessment (ABCDE), vital signs measurement, pain assessment, triage category assignment, resource allocation, initial interventions, staff notification

• **Outputs:** Triage Category 2 assignment, assessment form, vital signs documentation, pain score, bed assignment (Bay 2), clinical priority, physician notification

• **Customers:** Emergency physician, patient, ED staff, hospital bed management

------

## 4. Initial Emergency Physician Assessment

• **Suppliers:** Triaged patient, emergency physician, clinical equipment, patient's family, nursing staff

• **Inputs:** Patient with triage priority, assessment data, injury mechanism, patient symptoms, examination tools, ED protocols

• **Process:** Systematic trauma assessment, history taking, physical examination, diagnostic decision making, treatment orders, pain management, documentation

• **Outputs:** Physician assessment, clinical impression, diagnostic orders, treatment orders, medical documentation, family communication, consultation requests

• **Customers:** Patient, radiology department, laboratory, nursing staff, family, consulting specialists

------

## 5. X-ray Imaging Process

• **Suppliers:** Emergency physician, radiology staff (clerk, technologist), X-ray equipment, patient transport, radiology supplies

• **Inputs:** X-ray requisitions, patient with clinical indications, imaging equipment, positioning aids, imaging supplies, safety equipment

• **Process:** Order receipt, patient scheduling, transport, patient identification, positioning, safety measures, image acquisition, processing, quality assessment

• **Outputs:** X-ray images (chest, abdomen, forearm), quality assessment, images for interpretation, patient return, radiation documentation, completion notification

• **Customers:** Radiologist, emergency physician, patient, medical records, radiation safety officer

------

## 6. Radiology Interpretation

• **Suppliers:** Completed X-ray images, radiologist, previous studies, clinical information, radiology information system

• **Inputs:** X-ray images, clinical history, patient demographics, comparative studies, reporting templates, medical coding systems

• **Process:** Systematic image review, comparison analysis, findings documentation, critical findings identification, report generation, urgent communication, report transmission

• **Outputs:** Formal radiology reports, critical findings communication, diagnostic impressions, RIS documentation, report transmission, billing codes

• **Customers:** Emergency physician, patient, medical records, billing department, quality assurance

------

## 7. CT Scan Process

• **Suppliers:** Emergency physician, CT technologist, CT scanner equipment, contrast agents, patient monitoring equipment

• **Inputs:** CT requisition, patient requiring advanced imaging, IV contrast, CT equipment, monitoring devices, radiation safety protocols

• **Process:** Patient preparation, consent obtaining, contrast preparation, positioning, protocol selection, multi-phase imaging, reconstruction, quality assessment, monitoring

• **Outputs:** CT images (chest, abdomen, pelvis), image data, contrast records, radiation documentation, monitoring records, preliminary findings

• **Customers:** Radiologist, emergency physician, patient, surgical teams, radiation safety officer

------

## 8. CT Scan Interpretation

• **Suppliers:** Completed CT images, radiologist, PACS workstation, clinical information, reporting system

• **Inputs:** CT images, clinical history, previous studies, laboratory results, interpretation protocols, communication pathways

• **Process:** Systematic image review, multi-planar reconstruction, comparison analysis, critical findings identification, report generation, immediate communication, report finalization

• **Outputs:** Comprehensive CT report, critical findings communication, injury grading, treatment recommendations, medical documentation, surgeon notification

• **Customers:** Emergency physician, surgical team, patient and family, ICU team, medical records

------

## 9. Surgical Consultation

• **Suppliers:** Radiologist with findings, general and orthopedic surgeons, emergency physician, consultation protocols, OR scheduling

• **Inputs:** Critical CT findings, stable patient, surgical expertise, OR schedules, anesthesia availability, ICU bed availability

• **Process:** Surgical assessment, imaging review, treatment decisions, risk-benefit analysis, multidisciplinary consultation, patient counseling, treatment planning

• **Outputs:** Surgical consultation report, conservative treatment plan, ICU admission orders, orthopedic scheduling, patient education, care coordination

• **Customers:** Patient, ICU team, orthopedic surgery, family, case management

------

## 10. ICU Admission Process

• **Suppliers:** Surgical team, ICU staff, bed management, monitoring equipment, pharmacy

• **Inputs:** ICU admission orders, patient requiring monitoring, bed availability, monitoring equipment, nursing protocols, medication orders

• **Process:** Bed preparation, patient transfer, nursing assessment, equipment setup, care plan development, admission documentation, family education

• **Outputs:** Patient admitted to ICU, continuous monitoring established, nursing care plan, admission documentation, family education, team communication

• **Customers:** Patient, ICU medical team, family, billing department, case management

------

## 11. Administrative and Documentation

• **Suppliers:** All clinical departments, medical records staff, billing specialists, PhilHealth requirements, quality coordinators

• **Inputs:** Clinical documentation, diagnostic results, treatment records, insurance information, regulatory requirements, quality metrics

• **Process:** Record compilation, diagnosis coding, case rate determination, claims preparation, quality documentation, regulatory reporting, revenue cycle management

• **Outputs:** Complete medical record, coded diagnoses, PhilHealth claim, insurance billing, quality metrics, regulatory compliance

• **Customers:** Patient, insurance providers, regulatory bodies, hospital administration, legal departments

------

## Summary: Overall Process SIPOC

• **Suppliers:** Community (witnesses, transport), patient/family (history, insurance), clinical staff (expertise), support services (registration, billing), technology systems

• **Inputs:** Patient with trauma injuries, demographics and insurance, clinical expertise, diagnostic equipment, protocols and guidelines, hospital infrastructure

• **Process:** Assessment and triage, diagnosis through imaging, medical and surgical treatment, intensive monitoring, documentation and billing

• **Outputs:** Accurate diagnosis and treatment, safe effective care, complete documentation, regulatory compliance, quality metrics

• **Customers:** Patient and family (primary), medical team (ongoing care), hospital departments (administrative), external parties (insurance, regulators), community (public health)

## Key Insights from SIPOC Analysis

### Critical Success Factors

• **Information flow:** Accurate, timely data transmission • **Resource coordination:** Appropriate staff and equipment allocation • **Communication:** Effective handoffs and updates • **Quality control:** Error prevention and safety measures • **Documentation:** Complete, accurate record keeping

### Major Dependencies

• **Technology systems:** Reliable equipment and information systems • **Clinical expertise:** Qualified staff availability • **Resource availability:** Beds, equipment, supplies • **Communication systems:** Effective information sharing • **Regulatory compliance:** Complete documentation requirements
