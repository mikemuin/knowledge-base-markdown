# Pandoc - Complete HIMSS EMRAM 2009

------

## Overview: HIMSS EMRAM Model

- **Purpose:** Systematic framework for Electronic Medical Record adoption
- **Stages:** 8 progressive levels (Stage 0 to Stage 7)
- **Focus:** Patient safety, clinical quality, and operational efficiency
- **2009 Enhancement:** Added Stage 7 for advanced analytics and population health
- **Philippine Context:** Aligned with DOH strategies and PhilHealth modernization

------

## Key Differences: 2005 vs 2009 Model

- **2005 Model:** 7 stages (0-6) with Stage 6 as pinnacle
- **2009 Model:** 8 stages (0-7) with advanced analytics focus
- New Capabilities:
  - Population health management
  - Advanced clinical analytics
  - Predictive modeling and risk stratification
  - Clinical research and outcomes measurement
  - Complete paperless operations

------

## Stage 0: Paper-Based Foundation

- **Intent:** Pre-Digital Baseline - Complete Paper-Based Operations
- Key Characteristics:
  - No electronic clinical systems
  - Manual chart documentation
  - Paper-based order forms
  - Telephone/verbal communication
- Philippine Context:
  - Common in rural hospitals and district facilities
  - Limited IT budget and technical expertise
  - Traditional workflow comfort zone

------

## Stage 0: Clinician Capabilities

- **Physicians:** Write all orders by hand on paper forms
- **Nurses:** Document patient care using paper charts and flow sheets
- **Laboratory:** Results delivered via phone calls or paper printouts
- **Radiology:** Reports distributed as physical copies
- **Pharmacy:** Receive handwritten prescriptions for processing
- **Communication:** Relies on verbal reports and written notes

------

## Stage 1: Foundation - Complete Ancillary Digitization

- **Intent:** Establish Electronic Data Foundation Through 100% Digitization of Core Ancillary Services
- Key Systems:
  - Laboratory Information System (LIS)
  - Pharmacy Information System (PIS)
  - Radiology Information System (RIS)
- **Strategic Value:** Creates reliable electronic data streams for future EMR stages

------

## Stage 1: Features and Capabilities

- Features:
  - LIS with specimen tracking
  - PIS with inventory management
  - RIS with scheduling and reporting
  - Digital result storage and retrieval
- Clinician Capabilities:
  - Electronic specimen tracking from collection to result
  - Medication inventory and dispensing management
  - Imaging study scheduling and digital reporting
  - Electronic result access versus phone/paper delivery

------

## Stage 1: Philippine Healthcare Context

- **Implementation Level:** Tertiary hospitals and larger private medical centers
- Regulatory Alignment:
  - Essential for DOH accreditation
  - Supports PhilHealth compliance
  - Foundation for electronic claiming
- Benefits:
  - Improved departmental efficiency
  - Reduced manual errors
  - Better inventory management
  - Enhanced data availability

------

## Stage 2: Integration - Single Source of Truth Creation

- **Intent:** Create Integrated Clinical Data Repository with Standardized Vocabulary and Basic Clinical Decision Support
- Key Components:
  - Clinical Data Repository (CDR)
  - Controlled Medical Vocabulary
  - HL7 Integration
  - Basic Clinical Decision Support
- **Transformation:** From departmental silos to unified clinical information

------

## Stage 2: Features and Capabilities

- Features:
  - CDR with centralized data storage
  - HL7 integration between systems
  - Drug-drug and drug-allergy interaction alerts
  - Integrated results viewing interface
- Clinician Capabilities:
  - Single interface access to all lab, pharmacy, radiology results
  - Automatic medication interaction alerts
  - Immediate allergy warnings during prescribing
  - Integrated reporting across multiple services

------

## Stage 2: Strategic Impact

- **Data Integration:** Eliminates information silos
- **Standardization:** Consistent terminology and coding (ICD-10, CPT)
- **Patient Safety:** Automated clinical alerts and warnings
- **Quality Foundation:** Enables systematic measurement and improvement
- Philippine Context:
  - Critical for JCI accreditation
  - Supports PhilHealth standardized coding
  - Aligns with DOH Health Information System Strategy

------

## Stage 3: Documentation - Comprehensive Care Recording

- **Intent:** Enable Multidisciplinary Clinical Documentation and Care Coordination Across All Patient Care Areas
- Key Components:
  - Electronic nursing documentation
  - Digital clinical flow sheets
  - Electronic Medication Administration Record (EMAR)
  - Radiology Picture Archiving and Communication System (R-PACS)

------

## Stage 3: Features and Capabilities

- Features:
  - Electronic nursing documentation system
  - Digital vital signs and intake/output tracking
  - EMAR with medication verification
  - R-PACS for medical imaging access
- Clinician Capabilities:
  - Real-time electronic patient assessment documentation
  - Digital vital signs and measurement recording
  - Electronic medication administration tracking
  - Direct medical image viewing from clinical workstations

------

## Stage 3: Clinical Impact

- **Care Continuity:** Seamless information sharing across shifts
- **Team Coordination:** Improved multidisciplinary collaboration
- **Patient Safety:** Real-time access to current patient status
- Philippine Context:
  - Advanced capability for major medical centers
  - Supports nursing education programs

  - Aligns with Philippine Nursing Association standards

------

## Stage 4: Safety - Medication Order Transformation

- **Intent:** Eliminate Medication Errors Through Physician Electronic Prescribing with Clinical Decision Support
- **Focus:** Computerized Practitioner Order Entry (CPOE) for pharmacy orders only
- **Strategic Priority:** Address highest-risk, highest-frequency preventable errors
- **Change Management:** Significant physician workflow adaptation required

------

## Stage 4: Features and Capabilities

- Features:
  - CPOE for pharmacy orders
  - Real-time drug interaction checking
  - Allergy checking with prescribing alerts
  - Electronic prescription routing to pharmacy
- Clinician Capabilities:
  - Electronic medication prescribing with automatic legibility
  - Real-time clinical decision support during prescribing
  - Instant order delivery to pharmacy
  - Medication order status tracking from prescription to dispensing

------

## Stage 4: Safety Benefits

- **Error Prevention:** Addresses largest source of preventable medical errors
- Quality Improvement:
  - Dramatic reduction in medication errors
  - Improved order legibility and completeness
  - Enhanced drug interaction and allergy alerts
- Philippine Context:
  - Critical for international accreditation
  - Supports PhilHealth medication management initiatives
  - Essential for teaching hospitals and resident training

------

## Stage 5: Standardization - Complete Order Management

- **Intent:** Achieve Comprehensive Clinical Order Standardization with Evidence-Based Decision Support Protocols
- **Expansion:** CPOE for ALL clinical orders (lab, radiology, nursing, dietary)
- **Advanced Features:** Evidence-based order sets and clinical protocols
- **Impact:** Transform from individual preference to standardized care

------

## Stage 5: Features and Capabilities

- Features:
  - Complete CPOE for all clinical services
  - Evidence-based order sets and clinical protocols
  - Clinical pathway integration
  - Advanced clinical decision support with protocols
- Clinician Capabilities:
  - Single interface ordering for all clinical services
  - Utilization of evidence-based order sets for common conditions
  - Protocol-based recommendations for diagnosis-specific care
  - Structured care plans with automated intervention reminders

------

## Stage 5: Organizational Benefits

- **Care Standardization:** Evidence-based protocols across all services
- **Efficiency Optimization:** Streamlined ordering through intelligent order sets
- **Resource Management:** Optimized utilization of clinical resources

## Progressive Capability Enhancement

- **Stage 0 → 1:** From Manual to Digital Data
- **Stage 1 → 2:** From Silos to Integration
- **Stage 2 → 3:** From Viewing to Documenting
- **Stage 3 → 4:** From Documentation to Safe Ordering
- **Stage 4 → 5:** From Medication Safety to Complete Care Standardization
- **Stage 5 → 6:** From Standardization to Optimization
- **Stage 6 → 7:** From Optimization to Excellence
