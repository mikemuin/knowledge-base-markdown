# SIPOC Analysis: Trauma Care Clinical Workflow

## Complete Analysis of Miguel Santos Case Scenario

------

## 1. Accident Response and Transport

### Suppliers

- Accident witnesses and Good Samaritans
- Community members providing assistance
- Jeepney driver willing to transport
- Patient's family (wife Maria with documents)
- Emergency hotline operators

### Inputs

- Patient with traumatic injuries
- Accident scene assessment information
- Patient identification and medical history
- PhilHealth membership card and documents
- Transportation vehicle (jeepney)
- Basic first aid knowledge from bystanders

### Process

- Initial accident assessment and patient stabilization
- Decision making for transport method
- Community mobilization and assistance
- Patient loading and positioning for transport
- Continuous monitoring during transport
- Communication with family and hospital

### Outputs

- Patient transported to appropriate healthcare facility
- Basic vital status information
- Preliminary injury assessment
- Family notification and mobilization
- Documentation of accident circumstances
- Estimated time of hospital arrival

### Customers

- Patient requiring emergency medical care
- Hospital emergency department expecting patient
- Family members needing information and support
- Insurance providers requiring accident documentation

## 3. Emergency Department Triage

### Suppliers

- Registered patient from registration process
- Triage nurse with clinical assessment skills
- Triage protocols and guidelines
- Monitoring equipment (vital signs)
- Philippine Triage Scale standards

### Inputs

- Patient presenting with trauma injuries
- Initial vital signs and basic assessment
- Mechanism of injury information
- Patient pain level and symptoms
- Clinical triage protocols
- Emergency department capacity status

### Process

- Primary assessment (ABCDE approach)
- Vital signs measurement and documentation
- Pain assessment and scoring
- Triage category assignment using Philippine Triage Scale
- Resource allocation and bed assignment
- Initial emergency interventions
- Notification of appropriate clinical staff

### Outputs

- Triage category assignment (Category 2 - Urgent)
- Completed triage assessment form
- Vital signs documentation
- Pain assessment score
- Bed assignment (Emergency Bay 2)
- Clinical priority designation
- Notification to emergency physician

### Customers

- Emergency physician requiring prioritized patients
- Patient needing appropriate level of care
- Emergency department staff for workflow coordination
- Hospital bed management for resource allocation

------

## 4. Initial Emergency Physician Assessment

### Suppliers

- Triaged patient from triage process
- Emergency physician with trauma experience
- Clinical examination equipment
- Patient's family providing history
- Nursing staff for assistance

### Inputs

- Patient with assigned triage priority
- Triage assessment and vital signs
- Mechanism of injury details
- Patient symptoms and complaints
- Clinical examination tools

### Process

- Physical examination of all body systems

- Initial treatment order generation

### Outputs

### Customers

### Process

### Outputs

- Completed X-ray images (chest, abdomen, forearm)
- Technical quality assessment
- Images prepared for radiologist interpretation
- Patient returned to emergency care
- Radiation dose documentation
- Imaging completion notification

### Customers

- Radiologist requiring images for interpretation
- Emergency physician awaiting results
- Patient needing diagnostic information
- Medical records for permanent documentation
- Radiation safety officer for dose monitoring

- **Clinical Staff:** Physicians, nurses, technologists
- **Support Services:** Registration, billing, medical records
- **Technology Systems:** Imaging equipment, information systems

### High-Level Inputs

- **Patient:** Trauma injuries requiring emergency care
- **Information:** Demographics, insurance, medical history
- **Resources:** Clinical expertise, diagnostic equipment

- **Assessment:** Triage and clinical evaluation

### High-Level Outputs

## Key Insights from SIPOC Analysis

### Critical Success Factors

- **Information flow:** Accurate, timely data transmission between processes
- **Resource coordination:** Appropriate allocation of staff and equipment
- **Communication:** Effective handoffs and status updates
- **Quality control:** Error prevention and safety measures
- **Documentation:** Complete, accurate record keeping

### Major Dependencies

- **Technology systems:** Reliable equipment and information systems
- **Clinical expertise:** Qualified staff available when needed
- **Resource availability:** Beds, equipment, supplies accessible
- **Communication systems:** Effective information sharing methods
- **Regulatory compliance:** Meeting all documentation requirements

### Improvement Opportunities

- **Automation:** Reduce manual processes and improve efficiency
- **Integration:** Better system connectivity and data sharing
- **Standardization:** Consistent processes across all departments
- **Communication:** Real-time status updates and notifications
- **Resource optimization:** Better scheduling and utilization
