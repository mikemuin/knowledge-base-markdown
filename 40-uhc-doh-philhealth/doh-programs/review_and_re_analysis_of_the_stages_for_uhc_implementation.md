# Review and Re-analysis of the Stages for UHC Implementation

You are correct to seek a review and re-analysis of the stages for Universal Health Care (UHC) implementation, focusing on what is truly **achievable** and **practical** at each step. Drawing on the provided sources, a **maturity model** approach is indeed the guiding framework for UHC realization in the Philippines. This model breaks down the complex UHC implementation into **progressive milestones** across key "tracks" or "building blocks" of a health system.

While the sources refer to the "Local Health Systems Maturity Levels (LHS ML)" with three defined levels (Preparatory, Organizational, Functional), we can reframe these foundational stages to emphasize an active progression towards UHC realization, ensuring each stage outlines **doable and practical achievements**. The **8-Point Action Agenda** serves as the overarching strategic framework guiding these efforts.

The six core "tracks" or "building blocks" for this model are based on the World Health Organization's health system framework:

1. **Leadership and Governance**
2. **Financing**
3. **Health Workforce**
4. **Information**
5. **Medical Products, Vaccines, and Technologies**
6. **Service Delivery**

------

### Foundational Stages for UHC Realization

#### Stage 1: Foundational Readiness

This initial stage focuses on **readiness, formal commitments, and baseline assessments** crucial for initiating health system reforms and integration. It involves understanding the current state and making the necessary foundational agreements.

- Leadership & Governance Track:
  - **Provinces, Highly Urbanized Cities (HUCs), and Independent Component Cities (ICCs) formally commit to integrating their local health systems** into Province-wide and City-wide Health Systems (P/CWHS). This involves engaging LGUs through advocacies and orientations on the UHC Act and national policy goals.
  - **Baseline assessment of LGUsâ€™ health capacity** and corresponding investment needs is conducted. This includes inventory and mapping of health facilities, assessment of provider capacities, population profiling, and assessment of management support systems (referral, Disaster Risk Reduction and Management for Health [DRRM-H], epidemiologic surveillance, information systems, health promotion programs).
  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is created**, formalized by a P/CHB resolution and the opening of a dedicated bank account.
- Financing Track:
  - Baseline assessment of LGU counterpart and expenditure for health is conducted, typically as part of the overall LGU capacity assessment.
- Health Workforce Track:
  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for Human Resources for Health (HRH)** are completed. This includes distribution, staffing patterns (filled and unfilled positions by hiring authority), and learning and development needs.
- Information Track:
  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for health information management/ICT systems** are conducted, including the state of ICT governance.
- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track:
  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for procurement and supply chain management** are performed.
- Service Delivery Track:
  - **Review of existing referral systems** is conducted, including service capabilities and available health services.
  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for the Disaster Risk Reduction and Management for Health (DRRM-H) System** are performed.
  - **Baseline assessment on functional health literacy for health promotion programs** is undertaken for the catchment population.

#### Stage 2: System Development & Capacity Building

This stage focuses on **establishing network-wide guidelines, resources, and management structures** to facilitate and sustain the integration of local health systems, reflecting key **reforms provided under the UHC Act**. It is about building the necessary components for UHC.

- Leadership & Governance Track:
  - **Health Care Provider Networks (HCPNs)**, with **Primary Care Provider Networks (PCPNs) as their foundation**, are organized to deliver a continuum of care.
  - The **P/CWHS is formally organized**, with expanded P/CHB membership and functions, and a strengthened Provincial/City Health Office (P/CHO) including the establishment of technical divisions, an Epidemiology and Surveillance Unit (ESU), and a Health Promotion Unit (HPU).
  - The **Local Investment Plan for Health (LIPH) and Annual Operational Plans (AOPs) are formulated and approved by the P/CHB**, serving as strategic plans and bases for national grants.
- Financing Track:
  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is exclusively utilized for health programs, projects, and activities**.
  - PhilHealth prepares for and **pilots the contracting of HCPNs**, moving towards **performance-driven, closed-end, prospective payment mechanisms** like **capitation and global budgets** for primary care.
  - PhilHealth begins to implement a **comprehensive outpatient benefit**, including outpatient drug benefit and emergency medical services, in accordance with Health Technology Assessment Council (HTAC) recommendations, as mandated to begin within two years from the IRR's effectivity. The **Konsulta Package** is designed to progressively evolve into this comprehensive outpatient benefit.
- Health Workforce Track:
  - An **HRH plan is developed and integrated into the Local Investment Plan for Health (LIPH)**, covering staffing, HRH sharing, and incremental creation of positions.
  - **Reorientation of health professional education curricula towards primary health care commences**.
  - Guidelines for **Primary Care Worker (PCW) certification** begin development.
- Information Track:
  - A **health information management/ICT development plan for the entire P/CWHS is established**.
  - **Functional Electronic Medical Record (EMR) systems are implemented** among health facilities within the P/CWHS, capable of submitting reports to DOH/PhilHealth.
  - Initial implementation of **Integrated Health Information System Implementation Model (iHISIM)** clinical modules (Stage 1 within 1-18 months, e.g., EMR, referral system, telemedicine) is underway.
  - **Planning for the National Health Data Repository (NHDR) begins**, focusing on establishing national health data standards for interoperability.
- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track:
  - A **procurement plan for the entire P/CWHS is developed**.
  - The **Health Technology Assessment (HTA) process is institutionalized**, and the **Health Technology Assessment Council (HTAC) is established within one year from the UHC Act's effectivity**. Existing health benefit packages are rationalized within two years from HTAC establishment.
- Service Delivery Track:
  - **Updated technical guidelines/manuals on the referral system are in place**.
  - The **Disaster Risk Reduction and Management for Health (DRRM-H) Program is formally adopted** through a P/CHB Resolution.
  - Proactive and effective **health promotion programs and campaigns are adopted by P/CWHS**, with Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) recognized as on-the-ground health promotion officers; at least **one (1) module from the Local Health System Health Promotion Playbook is implemented**.

#### Stage 3: Service Operationalization & Sustained Integration

This stage is characterized by the **operationalization and consistent delivery** of integrated health services and functional management support systems, with an emphasis on **monitoring for functionality and impact**. It demonstrates the health system is actively working and achieving results.

- Leadership & Governance Track:
  - The **P/CWHS is institutionalized and actively monitored for performance**, ideally contracted by DOH and PhilHealth. Regular monitoring and evaluation of UHC implementation are performed using tools like the **Local Health Systems Maturity Levels (LHS ML)** and **LGU Health Scorecard**.
- Financing Track:
  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is exclusively utilized for health services and health system development**.
  - PhilHealth has **fully shifted to performance-driven, closed-end, prospective payment mechanisms** (e.g., capitation, global budget) for individual-based health services, moving away from traditional fee-for-service models.
  - The **Konsulta Package has fully transitioned into the Comprehensive Outpatient Benefit Package (COBP)** and is actively provided by contracted HCPNs, ensuring equitable access without financial hardship. This also includes the operationalization of **PhilHealth GAMOT (Guaranteed Accessible Medications for Outpatient Treatment)** for essential medicines.
- Health Workforce Track:
  - There is **increased availability of adequately and equitably distributed health workers** across facilities and communities.
  - Only individuals **certified by the DOH and the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) are eligible to be primary care providers**, as targeted within ten years from the UHC Act's effectivity.
- Information Track:
  - **Validated electronic medical record (EMR) systems effectively link PCPNs to secondary and tertiary care providers** within the P/CWHS.
  - The Integrated Health Information System Implementation Model (iHISIM) progresses to Stage 2 (non-clinical modules) and Stage 3, where the **iHIS integrates with the Philippine Health Information Exchange (PHIE)** within 34-36 months of the iHIS guidelines.
  - The **National Health Data Repository (NHDR) is operational** as the single point of submission and authoritative repository of health data, with all health-related entities complying with national health data standards for interoperability. All health and health-related data are integrated, processed, and analyzed for insights to support planning.
- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track:
  - An **interoperable electronic supply chain/logistics management information system is in place**.
  - **Health Technology Assessment (HTA) is fully institutionalized** as a fair and transparent priority setting mechanism, recommending funding and coverage decisions based on evidence. Prices of essential medicines are reduced (e.g., through Maximum Retail Price regulation, central price negotiation).
- Service Delivery Track:
  - The P/CWHS is actively delivering a **continuum of care**, with primary care serving as the foundation and point of initial contact.
  - **Fully functional and coordinated patient navigation and referral systems** are established within the HCPN.
  - The **DRRM-H system is functional**, evidenced by approved plans, organized and trained health emergency response teams, available commodities, and a functional operations center.
  - At least **three (3) modules from the Local Health System Health Promotion Playbook are implemented**, with annual reports/portfolios on health promotion accomplishments. This fosters a health-literate population that consistently makes healthy choices within health-enabling environments and under health-supportive governance.

### Final Stage: Universal Health Care Realization

This is the ultimate vision, as mandated by the UHC Act, signifying the achievement of a **comprehensive, integrated, and people-centered health system** where:

- **All Filipinos are guaranteed equitable access to quality and affordable health care goods and services and are protected against financial risk**.
- **Better health outcomes are universally achieved**, indicating an improved overall health status of the population.
- The health system is **responsive to the needs of the people**, consistently demonstrating efficiency, equity, and high quality of care.
- All local health systems are **fully integrated** as Province-wide and City-wide Health Systems (P/CWHS) across the country, ideally mandated by an Executive Order following a positive independent evaluation. This is targeted for **2027**.
- A **national interoperable digital health ecosystem is fully operational**, with the **NHDR serving as the single source of truth** for all health and health-related data, facilitating evidence-informed policy and planning.
- Health promotion and disease prevention are deeply embedded, fostering a **health-literate population** that consistently makes healthy choices within **health-enabling environments** and under **health-supportive governance**. The **8-Point Action Agenda** provides the strategic framework and specific interventions to guide the DOH and all stakeholders towards these ultimate goals.
