# Key Performance Indicators: Data Source Mapping (Philippine NOH 2023-2028 Context)

**Health Impact Indicators: Better Health Outcomes**

1. **Average Life Expectancy**
   - Initial Source: Civil Registration (paper, hospital forms)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA Reports
   - Final Source: Data Warehouse (PSA Vital Statistics System)
   - Barrier: Incomplete or delayed registration of deaths and births
   - High-Impact Action: Digitize and integrate LGU civil registry systems with hospital EMRs
2. **Prevalence of Stunting among Under-5s**
   - Initial Source: Community surveys, RHUs (paper or EMR)
   - Intermediate Source: Expanded National Nutrition Survey (ENNS)
   - Final Source: National Nutrition Council (NNC) data warehouse
   - Barrier: Infrequent and fragmented nutrition surveillance
   - High-Impact Action: Deploy barangay-based digital nutrition tracking tools
3. **Infant & Maternal Mortality Rates**
   - Initial Source: Birth/death certificates (paper, hospital EMR)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA and NDHS reports
   - Final Source: PSA, DOH epidemiology systems
   - Barrier: Underreporting and misclassification of deaths
   - High-Impact Action: Implement mandatory maternal death reviews and link EMRs to civil registry
4. **TB Mortality**
   - Initial Source: RHU TB registries (paper), EMRs
   - Intermediate Source: DOH TB Program Reports
   - Final Source: National TB Information System / DOH Data Warehouse
   - Barrier: Incomplete death reporting from LGUs
   - High-Impact Action: Automate death matching between TB registry and civil registry
5. **Road Traffic Deaths**
   - Initial Source: Hospital ER reports, PNP reports (paper)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA Reports
   - Final Source: PSA data warehouse
   - Barrier: Data fragmentation between health and transport sectors
   - High-Impact Action: Establish integrated trauma and injury surveillance system
6. **Premature NCD Mortality Rate**
   - Initial Source: Hospital reports (EMR/paper)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA Cause-of-Death registry
   - Final Source: PSA mortality data warehouse
   - Barrier: Low screening coverage and follow-up at primary care
   - High-Impact Action: Launch national NCD screening and follow-up initiative in RHUs

**Health System Indicators**

1. **Ambulatory Care-Sensitive Conditions in Hospitals**
   - Initial Source: Hospital discharge reports (EMR/paper)
   - Intermediate Source: Hospital administrative datasets
   - Final Source: DOH data warehouse
   - Barrier: Weak gatekeeping and referral enforcement
   - High-Impact Action: Enforce PCP referrals through PhilHealth claims validation
2. **IHR Joint External Evaluation Score**
   - Initial Source: DOH program performance (paper + EMR + digital)
   - Intermediate Source: DOH, WHO reports
   - Final Source: DOH/WHO data repositories
   - Barrier: Fragmented public health emergency response systems
   - High-Impact Action: Institutionalize national and LGU public health emergency units
3. **UHC Service Coverage Index**
   - Initial Source: Surveys, PhilHealth claims (paper/EMR)
   - Intermediate Source: WHO/PhilHealth assessment reports
   - Final Source: WHO SDG/UHC databases
   - Barrier: Inconsistent health service delivery and reporting across facilities
   - High-Impact Action: Standardize benefit packages and reporting across all provider levels

------

**Service Delivery & Financial Protection**

1. **Responsiveness Score / Client Satisfaction**
   - Initial Source: Patient surveys (paper, digital forms)
   - Intermediate Source: DOH or contracted firm reports
   - Final Source: DOH PMSMD data warehouse
   - Barrier: No regular national rollout of client experience surveys
   - High-Impact Action: Institutionalize client satisfaction survey as part of licensing/accreditation
2. **Out-of-pocket Health Spending**
   - Initial Source: Family surveys (FIES - paper)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA Family Income and Expenditure Survey
   - Final Source: PSA/DOH national health accounts
   - Barrier: Inadequate coverage for outpatient and diagnostics
   - High-Impact Action: Expand and increase PhilHealth coverage of outpatient services
3. **Incidence of Catastrophic Health Spending**
   - Initial Source: Household surveys (FIES)
   - Intermediate Source: PSA-processed microdata
   - Final Source: DOH Policy & Planning database
   - Barrier: PhilHealth reimbursement gaps
   - High-Impact Action: Reform PhilHealth case rates to match actual cost of care

- **Action Agenda 1 (Primary Care Access and PCP Registration)**
  - Barrier: Low registration with PCPs and low Konsulta provider engagement
  - High-Impact Action: Streamline and mandate PCP registration through barangay campaigns

  - Barrier: Inconsistent EMR adoption and lack of interoperability
  - High-Impact Action: Implement nationwide EMR certification and HIE onboarding
- **Action Agenda 4 (Disaster and Crisis Readiness)**
  - Barrier: Weak LGU DRRM-H institutionalization
  - High-Impact Action: Fund and staff local DRRM-H units and simulate response annually
- **Action Agenda 5 (Health Promotion & Literacy)**
  - Barrier: Fragmented health promotion workforce and unclear roles
  - High-Impact Action: Deploy HP Officers in every province and integrate HP plans in LIPH
- **Action Agenda 6 (Mental Health and Well-being)**
  - Barrier: Shortage of MH-trained personnel in primary care
  - High-Impact Action: Embed MNS services and screening in Konsulta and RHUs
- **Action Agenda 7 (Health Workforce Development)**
  - Barrier: HRH deployment and retention in remote areas
  - High-Impact Action: Expand NHWSS and reform HRH local compensation guidelines
- **Action Agenda 8 (Surveillance and Laboratory Systems)**
  - Barrier: Non-functional ESUs and laboratory response gaps
  - High-Impact Action: Fund and operationalize ESUs and public health labs in all provinces
