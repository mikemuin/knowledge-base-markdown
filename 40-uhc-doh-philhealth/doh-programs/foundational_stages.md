The foundational stages for Universal Health Care (UHC) alignment are designed as a **maturity model**, specifically the **Local Health Systems Maturity Levels (LHS ML)**, to guide the **progressive realization** of UHC in the Philippines. This approach addresses practicality by breaking down the complex UHC implementation into **achievable milestones** across distinct **"tracks"** or **"building blocks"** of a health system.

There are **three foundational stages** leading to the **final stage of UHC Realization**. Each foundational stage defines **Key Result Areas (KRAs)** that must be achieved within six core "tracks" based on the World Health Organization's health system building blocks: **Leadership and Governance, Financing, Health Workforce, Information, Medical Products, Vaccines and Technologies, and Service Delivery**. The **8-Point Action Agenda** serves as the strategic framework guiding these efforts.

------

### Foundational Stages

#### Stage 1: Preparatory Level (LHS ML Level I)

This initial stage focuses on **readiness, formal commitments, and baseline assessments** essential for initiating health system reforms and integration.

- Leadership & Governance Track

:

  - Provinces, Highly Urbanized Cities (HUCs), and Independent Component Cities (ICCs) **formally commit to integrating their local health systems** into Province-wide and City-wide Health Systems (P/CWHS).
  - **Baseline assessment of LGUsâ€™ health capacity** and corresponding investment needs is conducted.

- Financing Track

:

  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is created**, formalized by a P/CHB resolution and the opening of a dedicated bank account.

- Health Workforce Track

:

  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for Human Resources for Health (HRH)**, including distribution, staffing patterns (filled and unfilled positions by hiring authority), and learning/development needs.

- Information Track

:

  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for health information management/ICT systems**, including the state of ICT governance.

- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track

:

  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for procurement and supply chain management**.

- Service Delivery Track

:

  - **Review of existing referral systems** (service capabilities and available health services).
  - **Baseline assessment, gaps analysis, and identification of investment needs for Disaster Risk Reduction and Management for Health (DRRM-H) System**.
  - **Baseline assessment on functional health literacy for health promotion programs**.

#### Stage 2: Organizational Level (LHS ML Level II)

This stage focuses on **establishing network-wide guidelines, resources, and management structures** to facilitate and sustain the integration of local health systems, reflecting key **reforms provided under the UHC Act**.

- Leadership & Governance Track

:

  - **Health Care Provider Networks (HCPNs)**, with **Primary Care Provider Networks (PCPNs) as their foundation**, are organized to deliver a continuum of care.
  - The **P/CWHS is formally organized**, with expanded P/CHB membership/functions and a strengthened Provincial/City Health Office (P/CHO) including the establishment of technical divisions, an Epidemiology and Surveillance Unit (ESU), and a Health Promotion Unit (HPU).

- Financing Track

:

  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is exclusively utilized for health programs, projects, and activities**.
  - PhilHealth prepares for and pilots the contracting of HCPNs, moving towards **performance-driven, closed-end, prospective payment mechanisms** like **capitation and global budgets** for primary care.

- Health Workforce Track

:

  - An **HRH plan is developed and integrated into the Local Investment Plan for Health (LIPH)**, covering staffing, HRH sharing, and incremental creation of positions.
  - **Reorientation of health professional education curricula towards primary health care** commences, and guidelines for **Primary Care Worker (PCW) certification** begin development.

- Information Track

:

  - A **health information management/ICT development plan** for the entire P/CWHS is established.
  - **Functional Electronic Medical Record (EMR) systems** are implemented among health facilities within the P/CWHS, capable of submitting reports to DOH/PhilHealth.
  - Initial implementation of **Integrated Health Information System (iHISIM)** clinical modules (Stage 1 within 1-18 months, e.g., EMR, referral system, telemedicine) is underway.
  - Planning for the **National Health Data Repository (NHDR)** begins, focusing on establishing national health data standards for interoperability.

- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track

:

  - A **procurement plan for the entire P/CWHS** is developed.
  - The **Health Technology Assessment (HTA) process is institutionalized**, and the **Health Technology Assessment Council (HTAC) is established within one year from the UHC Act's effectivity**.

- Service Delivery Track

:

  - **Updated technical guidelines/manuals on the referral system** are in place.
  - The **Disaster Risk Reduction and Management for Health (DRRM-H) Program is formally adopted** through a P/CHB Resolution.
  - Proactive and effective **health promotion programs and campaigns** are adopted by P/CWHS, with Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) recognized as on-the-ground health promotion officers.
  - The **Local Investment Plan for Health (LIPH) and Annual Operational Plans (AOPs)** are formulated and approved by the P/CHB, serving as strategic plans and bases for national grants.

#### Stage 3: Functional Level (LHS ML Level III)

This stage is characterized by the **operationalization and consistent delivery** of integrated health services and functional management support systems, with an emphasis on **monitoring for functionality and impact**.

- Leadership & Governance Track

:

  - The **P/CWHS is institutionalized and actively monitored** for performance, ideally contracted by DOH and PhilHealth.
  - Regular monitoring and evaluation of UHC implementation are performed using tools like the **Local Health Systems Maturity Levels (LHS ML)** and **LGU Health Scorecard**. As of 2023, **63 out of 71 UHC Integration Sites achieved at least 70% of LHS ML Level 2 KRAs**, demonstrating practical progress.

- Financing Track

:

  - The **Special Health Fund (SHF) is exclusively utilized for health services and health system development**.
  - PhilHealth has **fully shifted to performance-driven, closed-end, prospective payment mechanisms** (e.g., capitation, global budget) for individual-based health services, moving away from traditional fee-for-service models.
  - The **Konsulta Package has fully transitioned into the Comprehensive Outpatient Benefit Package (COBP)** and is actively provided by contracted HCPNs, ensuring equitable access without financial hardship. This also includes the operationalization of **PhilHealth GAMOT (Guaranteed Accessible Medications for Outpatient Treatment)** for essential medicines.

- Health Workforce Track

:

  - There is increased availability of **adequately and equitably distributed health workers** across facilities and communities.
  - Only individuals **certified by the DOH and the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) are eligible to be primary care providers**, as targeted within ten years from the UHC Act's effectivity.

- Information Track

:

  - **Validated electronic medical record (EMR) systems effectively link PCPNs to secondary and tertiary care providers** within the P/CWHS, indicating progress in digital transformation.
  - The Integrated Health Information System Implementation Model (iHISIM) progresses to Stage 2 (non-clinical modules) and Stage 3, where the **iHIS integrates with the Philippine Health Information Exchange (PHIE)** within 34-36 months of the iHIS guidelines.
  - The **National Health Data Repository (NHDR) is operational** as the single point of submission and authoritative repository of health data, with all health-related entities complying with national health data standards for interoperability. All health and health-related data are integrated, processed, and analyzed for insights to support planning.

- Medical Products, Vaccines, & Technologies Track

:

  - An **interoperable electronic supply chain/logistics management information system** is in place.
  - **Health Technology Assessment (HTA) is fully institutionalized** as a fair and transparent priority setting mechanism, recommending funding and coverage decisions based on evidence. Prices of essential medicines are reduced (e.g., through Maximum Retail Price regulation).

- Service Delivery Track

:

  - The P/CWHS is actively delivering a **continuum of care**, with primary care serving as the foundation and point of initial contact.
  - Fully functional and coordinated **patient navigation and referral systems** are established within the HCPN.
  - The **DRRM-H system is functional**, evidenced by approved plans, organized and trained health emergency response teams, available commodities, and a functional operations center.
  - At least **three modules from the Local Health System Health Promotion Playbook are implemented**, with annual reports/portfolios on health promotion accomplishments.

------

### Final Stage: Universal Health Care Realization

The ultimate vision, as mandated by the UHC Act, is to achieve a **comprehensive, integrated, and people-centered health system** where:

- **All Filipinos are guaranteed equitable access to quality and affordable health care goods and services and are protected against financial risk**.
- **Better health outcomes are universally achieved**, signifying an improved overall health status of the population.
- The health system is **responsive to the needs of the people**, consistently demonstrating efficiency, equity, and high quality of care.
- All local health systems are **fully integrated** as Province-wide and City-wide Health Systems (P/CWHS) across the country, ideally mandated by an Executive Order following a positive independent evaluation. This is targeted for **2027**.
- A **national interoperable digital health ecosystem** is fully operational, with the **NHDR serving as the single source of truth** for all health and health-related data, facilitating evidence-informed policy and planning.
- Health promotion and disease prevention are deeply embedded, fostering a **health-literate population** that consistently makes healthy choices within **health-enabling environments** and under **health-supportive governance**. The **8-Point Action Agenda** provides the strategic framework and specific interventions to guide the DOH and all stakeholders towards these ultimate goals.
