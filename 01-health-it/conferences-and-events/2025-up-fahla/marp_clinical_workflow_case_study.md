# Clinical Workflow Case Study

### From Accident to ICU: A Philippine Healthcare Journey

---

## Case Overview

**Patient:** Miguel Santos, 35-year-old motorcycle taxi driver
**Incident:** Motorcycle vs. Jeepney collision
**Location:** Provincial highway, Batangas
**Hospital:** Batangas Provincial Hospital (200-bed regional facility)
**Date:** Friday, May 13, 2025, 2:45 PM

**Key Question:** How does a trauma patient journey through the Philippine healthcare system, and where can we improve?

---

## The Accident (2:45 PM)

### What Happened

- Motorcycle taxi driver collides with jeepney on provincial highway
- Patient thrown from motorcycle, conscious but in severe pain
- Visible injuries: deformed left arm, chest/abdominal pain
- Good Samaritan calls for help

### Initial Response Challenges

- **Ambulance delay:** 30 minutes away
- **Transport decision:** 15 minutes to decide on jeepney transport
- **Common practice:** Private vehicle transport in provincial areas

**Time Lost: 15 minutes**

---

## Transport Phase (3:00-3:20 PM)

### Transport Details

- **Method:** Private jeepney with family assistance
- **Duration:** 20 minutes to hospital
- **Accompaniment:** Wife Maria with PhilHealth documents
- **Condition:** Patient remains conscious but deteriorating

### Provincial Reality

- Limited ambulance services in rural areas
- Family-supported transport systems
- Community assistance in emergencies

**Total Time Since Accident: 35 minutes**

---

## Hospital Arrival & Registration (3:20-3:35 PM)

### Registration Process

- **Patient identification:** Basic demographics collection
- **PhilHealth verification:** System delays, manual backup
- **Documentation:** Multiple forms, manual processes
- **Medical record creation:** New MRN assignment

### Pain Points Identified

- Slow internet affecting PhilHealth verification
- Multiple manual forms requiring duplicate data entry
- Incomplete accident information gathering

**Registration Time: 15 minutes | Total: 50 minutes**

---

## Emergency Department Triage (3:35-3:45 PM)

### Triage Assessment

- **Philippine Triage Scale:** Category 2 (Urgent - <30 minutes)
- **Vital signs:** Stable but concerning (HR 98, RR 22, Pain 8/10)
- **Primary assessment:** Obvious fracture, possible internal injuries
- **Resource allocation:** Emergency Bay 2, monitoring initiated

### Clinical Decision Points

- Mechanism of injury suggests high-energy trauma
- Multiple system involvement suspected
- Immediate imaging requirements identified

**Triage Time: 10 minutes | Total: 65 minutes**

---

## Initial Physician Assessment (3:45-4:05 PM)

### Systematic Evaluation

- **History:** High-speed collision, no loss of consciousness
- **Physical exam:** Left forearm fracture, chest/abdominal tenderness
- **Clinical assessment:** Hemodynamically stable, multiple injuries
- **Treatment orders:** Pain management, imaging studies, monitoring

### Diagnostic Strategy

- **Primary concern:** Rule out internal injuries
- **Imaging plan:** X-rays first, CT scan likely needed
- **Pain management:** Morphine for comfort and cooperation

**Assessment Time: 20 minutes | Total: 85 minutes**

---

## X-ray Imaging Process (4:05-5:15 PM)

### Workflow Steps

1. **Order completion:** Manual requisition forms (3 min)
2. **Order transmission:** Hand-carried to radiology (5 min)
3. **Scheduling delay:** Waiting for equipment availability (15 min)
4. **Patient transport:** Wheelchair to radiology (5 min)
5. **Image acquisition:** 3 separate studies (20 min)
6. **Film processing:** Automatic processor (8 min)
7. **Quality review:** Technical assessment (5 min)

### System Challenges

- Manual paper-based ordering system
- Physical transport of requisitions
- Equipment scheduling bottlenecks
- Film processing delays

**X-ray Process Time: 70 minutes | Total: 155 minutes**

---

## Radiology Interpretation (5:15-5:45 PM)

### Radiologist Findings

- **Chest X-ray:** Left 7th and 8th rib fractures
- **Abdominal X-ray:** No obvious abnormalities
- **Forearm X-ray:** Comminuted radius/ulna fractures

### Critical Communication

- **Immediate phone call:** Urgent findings to ED physician
- **Recommendation:** CT scan needed for internal injury evaluation
- **Documentation:** Formal written reports completed

### Decision Impact

- X-ray findings confirm need for advanced imaging
- Surgical consultation requirements identified

**Interpretation Time: 30 minutes | Total: 185 minutes**

---

## CT Scan Process (5:45-7:30 PM)

### CT Scan Workflow

1. **Clinical decision:** Based on X-ray findings (2 min)
2. **Order completion:** CT requisition with contrast (5 min)
3. **Scheduling delay:** One routine scan ahead (30 min)
4. **Patient preparation:** NPO status, IV access (25 min)
5. **Image acquisition:** Multi-phase contrast study (20 min)
6. **Technical review:** Quality assessment (5 min)

### Resource Constraints

- Limited CT availability
- Single scanner serving entire hospital
- Scheduling conflicts with routine studies
- Complex contrast protocols

**CT Process Time: 105 minutes | Total: 290 minutes**

---

## Critical CT Findings (7:30-8:00 PM)

### Radiologist Interpretation

- **Liver:** Grade II laceration with hemoperitoneum
- **Spleen:** Grade II laceration with perisplenic hematoma
- **Chest:** Rib fractures with small pleural effusion
- **Assessment:** Significant internal injuries requiring surgery consultation

### Immediate Actions

- **Critical call:** Immediate notification to ED physician
- **Surgeon notification:** General surgery consultation requested
- **Monitoring escalation:** Closer observation required

### Clinical Impact

- Diagnosis confirms serious internal injuries
- Treatment plan shifts to surgical consideration

**CT Interpretation Time: 30 minutes | Total: 320 minutes**

---

## Surgical Consultation (8:00-8:45 PM)

### Multidisciplinary Assessment

- **General surgeon:** Conservative management decision
- **Orthopedic surgeon:** Delayed surgical repair plan
- **Treatment strategy:** ICU monitoring vs. immediate surgery

### Clinical Decision Making

- **Conservative approach:** Stable vital signs, grade II injuries
- **Monitoring plan:** Serial exams and lab work
- **Surgical timing:** Forearm surgery in 24-48 hours

### Family Communication

- **Injury explanation:** Liver/spleen lacerations, fractures
- **Treatment plan:** ICU monitoring, delayed surgery
- **Prognosis discussion:** 5-7 day hospitalization expected

**Consultation Time: 45 minutes | Total: 365 minutes**

---

## ICU Admission (8:45-9:30 PM)

### Admission Process

- **Bed coordination:** ICU bed 3 assignment
- **Transfer orders:** Monitoring protocols established
- **Insurance processing:** PhilHealth coverage confirmed
- **Financial counseling:** Cost estimates provided

### Care Transition

- **Monitoring setup:** Continuous vital signs, hourly assessments
- **Documentation:** ICU flow sheet initiated
- **Family support:** Visiting arrangements, overnight accommodation

### Administrative Completion

- **PhilHealth:** ₱32,000 case rate coverage
- **Estimated costs:** ₱80,000-120,000 total
- **Documentation:** Trauma registry completion

**Admission Time: 45 minutes | Total: 410 minutes**

## Pain Point Analysis

### Process Delays

- **Transport decision:** 15 minutes initial delay
- **Registration:** 15 minutes for documentation
- **X-ray scheduling:** 15 minutes equipment wait
- **CT scheduling:** 30 minutes routine scan ahead
- **Multiple handoffs:** Communication delays throughout

### System Issues

- **Manual processes:** Paper forms, phone calls, physical transport
- **Limited integration:** No automatic order transmission
- **Communication gaps:** Delayed result reporting
- **Resource constraints:** Single CT scanner, limited staff

---

## Information Flow Problems

### Documentation Burden

- **Multiple forms:** Registration, triage, assessment, orders
- **Duplicate data entry:** Same information entered repeatedly
- **Manual transmission:** Physical transport of requisitions
- **Delayed verification:** PhilHealth system connectivity issues

### Communication Inefficiencies

- **Phone-based reporting:** Manual result communication
- **Missing information:** Incomplete accident details
- **Handoff delays:** Information lost in transitions
- **No real-time status:** Limited visibility into process status

---

## Philippine Healthcare Context

### Resource Constraints

- **Infrastructure:** Limited equipment, single CT scanner
- **Staffing:** One radiologist covering multiple hospitals
- **Technology:** Paper-based systems, poor integration
- **Connectivity:** Slow internet affecting PhilHealth verification

### Cultural Factors

- **Family involvement:** Spouse accompanies throughout process
- **Community support:** Good Samaritan assistance
- **Economic factors:** Financial counseling required
- **Geographic challenges:** Provincial location affects resources

---

## Technology Gap Analysis

### Current State Systems

- **Paper-based registration:** Manual forms and logbooks
- **Standalone imaging:** Film-based X-ray, separate CT workstation
- **Phone communication:** Verbal result reporting
- **Manual scheduling:** Paper-based appointment books
- **Disconnected billing:** Separate PhilHealth processing

### Integration Opportunities

- **Electronic ordering:** Automatic requisition transmission
- **Digital imaging:** PACS with immediate availability
- **Real-time communication:** Automated result notification
- **Integrated scheduling:** Online appointment management
- **Connected systems:** EMR-RIS-PACS integration

---

## Improvement Opportunities

### Quick Wins (0-3 months)

- **Standardized protocols:** Consistent trauma pathways
- **Mobile communication:** WhatsApp groups for urgent findings
- **Parallel processing:** Concurrent rather than sequential care

### Technology Solutions (3-18 months)

- **Electronic ordering:** CPOE system implementation

- **System integration:** HL7/DICOM connectivity

- **Automated scheduling:** Real-time availability systems

## Lessons Learned

### Workflow Analysis Insights

1. **Manual processes dominate** current healthcare delivery
2. **Poor system integration** creates significant delays
3. **Communication inefficiencies** affect patient care
4. **Resource bottlenecks** limit throughput
5. **Technology gaps** create workflow disruptions

### System Architecture Needs

1. **Standards-based integration** (HL7, DICOM)
2. **Real-time communication** capabilities
3. **Mobile accessibility** for clinical staff
4. **Automated scheduling** and resource management
5. **Comprehensive monitoring** and alerting
