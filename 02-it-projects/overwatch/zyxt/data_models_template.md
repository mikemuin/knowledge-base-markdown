## Instructions for Use

### General Guidelines

**Target Week Calculation:** To calculate the Target Week from a date:

1. Identify which week of the month the date falls in
2. Format as "Week X of [Month]"
3. Example: January 22, 2026 → "Week 4 of January"

**Internal Only Flag:** Check "Internal Only" for sensitive information that should not be shared with external stakeholders. Leave unchecked for items that can be openly communicated.

**Grouping/Categories:** Use groups to organize related items. Maximum 2 levels of hierarchy are supported (Parent Group → Child Group → Items).

### Module-Specific Guidelines

**Milestones:** Major stage gates in the project lifecycle. Mark "Critical Path" for make-or-break dates that define project success.

**Action Items:** High-level directives requiring execution. Priority is manually set based on PM judgment and context.

**Deliverables:** Tangible outputs required for project progression. Each should have clear acceptance criteria.

**Dependencies:** External constraints and blockers. Track the party/system being depended upon and maintain regular communication.

**Risks:** Potential future problems. Probability + Impact = Severity. Always include mitigation strategy and contingency plan.

**Issues:** Current problems requiring resolution. Issues are materialized risks or new problems that have occurred.

**Change Requests:** Formal requests to modify project scope, schedule, budget, or resources. Requires justification and impact assessment.

**Decisions:** Key choices made during the project. Include rationale and alternatives considered. Lock decisions after initial review period.

**Meeting Events:** Scheduled gatherings for collaboration and communication. Track agenda, attendees, and link to meeting minutes.

**Meeting Minutes:** Record of what was discussed and decided in meetings. Link to the parent meeting event.

**Journal Entries:** Timeline narrative of project activities. Can link to other entities (risks, decisions, milestones, etc.) for context.

**Links & References:** External resources and tools used by the project. Organize by category and maintain accessibility.

**Lists & Checklists:** Flexible tracking for tasks, requirements, or any itemized tracking needs. Check off items as completed.

**Lessons Learned:** Capture what worked and what didn't for organizational knowledge building. Mark items applicable to future projects.

**Status Reports:** Executive summaries using snapshot technology. Once published, reports are immutable and serve as historical record.

### Priority Levels

- **Low:** Nice to have, minimal impact if delayed
- **Medium:** Important but some flexibility exists
- **High:** Critical to timeline, should be prioritized
- **Critical:** Absolutely must be completed, project-blocking

### Status Values

**Execution Status (Milestones, Action Items, Deliverables):**

- **Not Started:** Identified but work hasn't begun
- **In Progress:** Actively being worked on
- **Completed:** Work finished and verified
- **Cancelled:** No longer needed or relevant
- **On Hold:** Temporarily paused

**Dependency Status:**

- **Identified:** Recognized and logged
- **Engaged:** Communication established with dependent party
- **In Progress:** Actively working toward resolution
- **Resolved:** Dependency cleared
- **Blocked:** Unable to proceed

**Risk Status:**

- **Identified:** Recognized and logged
- **Assessed:** Probability and impact evaluated
- **Mitigating:** Actions being taken to reduce risk
- **Monitoring:** Actively watching risk status
- **Closed:** Risk no longer relevant
- **Materialized:** Risk has occurred (becomes an issue)

**Issue Status:**

- **Open:** Issue identified and logged
- **In Progress:** Actively working on resolution
- **Resolved:** Solution implemented
- **Closed:** Verified and closed
- **Escalated:** Raised to higher authority

**Change Request Status:**

- **Submitted:** Request logged
- **Under Review:** Being evaluated
- **Approved:** Accepted for implementation
- **Rejected:** Not approved
- **Implemented:** Changes completed
- **Cancelled:** Request withdrawn

### Impact Levels

- **Low:** Minor inconvenience, workarounds available
- **Medium:** Moderate impact, requires attention
- **High:** Significant impact, urgent attention needed
- **Critical:** Severe impact, immediate action required

### RAG Status (Red/Amber/Green)

Manual assessment by PM based on professional judgment:

- **Red:** Significant issues, project at risk, immediate intervention needed
- **Amber:** Some concerns, careful monitoring required, may need support
- **Green:** On track, no major concerns, progressing as planned

### Notes and Comments

Use the Notes/Context sections to capture:

- Rationale for decisions made
- Changes to dates or priorities
- Dependencies and blockers
- Stakeholder feedback
- Updates and progress
- Lessons learned in real-time
