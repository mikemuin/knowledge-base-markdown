# Pandoc - What Are the Features of an EMR?

A Progressive Journey Through HIMSS EMRAM 2009

------

## Understanding EMR Features Through Progressive Stages

- **EMR Definition:** Electronic Medical Record system with evolving capabilities
- **Progressive Development:** Features build systematically from basic to advanced
- **HIMSS EMRAM Framework:** 8-stage model (0-7) showing feature evolution
- Feature Categories:
  - Data capture and storage
  - Integration and interoperability
  - Clinical decision support
  - Workflow optimization
  - Analytics and intelligence

------

## The Foundation: From Paper to Digital

- **Stage 0 Baseline:** Complete paper-based operations
- **No EMR Features:** Manual processes dominate all workflows
- The Transformation Goal:
  - Replace paper charts with electronic records
  - Automate clinical workflows
  - Enable data-driven decision making
  - Improve patient safety and quality
- **Journey Ahead:** Progressive feature enhancement through 7 stages

------

## Stage 1: Basic EMR Features - Departmental Systems

- **Strategic Intent:** Establish Electronic Data Foundation Through 100% Digitization of Core Ancillary Services
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Ancillary services generate most structured, standardized clinical data
  - Provides immediate ROI through operational efficiency
  - Creates reliable electronic data streams for future EMR development
  - Builds organizational confidence in electronic systems

------

## Stage 1: Core EMR Features Introduced

- Laboratory Information System (LIS):
  - Electronic specimen tracking and result generation
  - Automated lab workflow management
- Pharmacy Information System (PIS):
  - Digital medication inventory and dispensing tracking
  - Electronic prescription management
- Radiology Information System (RIS):
  - Imaging study scheduling and report generation
  - Digital radiology workflow automation

------

## Stage 1: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- Laboratory Staff:
  - Track specimens electronically from collection to result
  - Generate digital lab reports automatically
  - Access historical lab data instantly
- Pharmacists:
  - Manage medication inventory electronically
  - Track dispensing activities digitally
  - Generate medication profiles and reports
- Radiologists:
  - Schedule imaging studies electronically
  - Generate digital radiology reports
  - Access imaging study history

------

## Stage 1: Why This Foundation Matters

- **Intent Achieved:** Electronic data foundation established in critical departments
- Strategic Value:
  - First digital touchpoints create electronic data streams
  - Efficiency gains through automated departmental workflows
  - Error reduction by eliminating transcription mistakes
  - Foundation building for future EMR integration
- **Next Challenge:** These systems operate in silos - need integration

------

## Stage 2: Advanced EMR Features - Integration and Intelligence

- **Strategic Intent:** Create Integrated Clinical Data Repository with Standardized Vocabulary and Basic Clinical Decision Support
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Eliminates dangerous information silos between departments
  - Creates single source of truth for clinical decision-making
  - Introduces patient safety through automated alerts
  - Establishes data infrastructure for evidence-based medicine

------

## Stage 2: Integration Features Introduced

- **Clinical Data Repository (CDR):** Central storage for all clinical data from multiple sources
- **HL7 Integration:** Seamless data exchange between all systems
- **Controlled Medical Vocabulary:** Standardized terminology and coding (ICD-10, CPT)
- Basic Clinical Decision Support:
  - Drug-drug interaction alerts
  - Drug-allergy contraindication warnings
- **Integrated User Interface:** Single access point for all clinical data

------

## Stage 2: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- All Clinical Staff:
  - View lab, pharmacy, and radiology results from single interface
  - Access complete patient data from multiple departments instantly
  - Receive automatic safety alerts during clinical decisions
- Physicians:
  - Get immediate drug interaction warnings when prescribing
  - Access comprehensive patient medication history from all sources
- Quality Teams:
  - Generate standardized reports using controlled vocabulary
  - Access integrated data for systematic quality improvement

------

## Stage 2: Why Integration Transforms Care

- **Intent Achieved:** Single source of truth with intelligent safety features
- Strategic Impact:
  - Real-time clinical intelligence prevents medical errors
  - Comprehensive patient view enables better clinical decisions
  - Standardized data supports quality measurement and improvement
  - Platform created for advanced EMR capabilities
- **Next Challenge:** Need comprehensive clinical documentation beyond departmental data

------

## Stage 3: Comprehensive EMR Features - Clinical Documentation

- **Strategic Intent:** Enable Multidisciplinary Clinical Documentation and Care Coordination Across All Patient Care Areas
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Transforms episodic departmental data into continuous care monitoring
  - Enables real-time care coordination across multidisciplinary teams
  - Provides complete patient picture for clinical decision-making
  - Establishes foundation for medication administration safety

------

## Stage 3: Documentation Features Introduced

- **Electronic Nursing Documentation:** Digital patient assessments, care plans, and nursing notes
- **Clinical Flow Sheets:** Electronic vital signs, intake/output, and measurement tracking
- **Electronic Medication Administration Record (EMAR):** Complete medication administration tracking
- **Radiology PACS Integration:** Medical images accessible within EMR interface
- **Shift Reporting Capabilities:** Electronic handoff communication between care teams
- **Care Plan Management:** Structured, shareable care planning across disciplines

------

## Stage 3: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- Nurses:
  - Document patient assessments electronically in real-time
  - Record vital signs directly into digital flow sheets
  - Track medication administration with electronic verification
  - Provide comprehensive electronic shift reports with complete patient data
- Physicians:
  - Review complete nursing assessments and interventions within EMR
  - Access medical images directly from patient record interface
  - View current patient status and care plans instantly from any location

------

## Stage 3: Why Documentation Enables Care Coordination

- **Intent Achieved:** Complete clinical documentation with seamless care coordination
- Strategic Value:
  - Real-time patient information improves clinical decision-making
  - Electronic communication eliminates information gaps between shifts
  - Medication administration tracking enhances patient safety
  - Complete clinical picture supports comprehensive care planning
- **Next Challenge:** Physicians still writing orders by hand - highest safety risk

------

## Stage 4: Enhanced EMR Features - Electronic Prescribing

- **Strategic Intent:** Eliminate Medication Errors Through Physician Electronic Prescribing with Clinical Decision Support
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Medication errors are largest source of preventable medical errors
  - Handwritten prescriptions cause legibility and completeness issues
  - Electronic prescribing provides real-time safety verification
  - Physician workflow transformation achieves maximum patient safety impact

------

## Stage 4: Prescribing Features Introduced

- Computerized Practitioner Order Entry (CPOE) for Medications:
  - Electronic prescription generation with automatic legibility
  - Real-time drug interaction checking during prescribing
  - Allergy verification and contraindication alerts
  - Formulary management and cost information integration
- Enhanced Clinical Decision Support:
  - Medication dosing recommendations based on patient parameters
  - Drug-specific safety alerts and warnings
  - Clinical guideline integration during prescribing process

------

## Stage 4: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- Physicians:
  - Prescribe all medications electronically with guaranteed legibility and completeness
  - Receive real-time safety alerts for drug interactions and allergies during prescribing
  - Access formulary information and cost data to optimize medication selection
  - View complete medication interaction profiles before finalizing prescriptions
- Pharmacists:
  - Receive complete, legible medication orders instantly upon physician entry
  - Verify orders efficiently with full clinical context and automated safety checking
  - Process prescriptions faster with reduced clarification requirements

------

## Stage 4: Why Electronic Prescribing Revolutionizes Safety

- **Intent Achieved:** Dramatic reduction in medication errors through electronic verification
- Strategic Impact:
  - Eliminates handwriting-related prescription errors completely
  - Real-time clinical intelligence prevents dangerous drug interactions
  - Streamlined workflow improves physician efficiency and satisfaction
  - Systematic medication safety enhancement across entire organization
- **Next Challenge:** Other clinical orders (lab, radiology) still manual - need comprehensive ordering

------

## Stage 5: Complete EMR Features - Total Order Management

- **Strategic Intent:** Achieve Comprehensive Clinical Order Standardization with Evidence-Based Decision Support Protocols
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Building on medication ordering success to standardize all clinical processes
  - Evidence-based protocols ensure consistent, high-quality care delivery
  - Comprehensive ordering eliminates variability in clinical practice
  - Intelligent order sets optimize resource utilization and clinical outcomes

------

## Stage 5: Complete Ordering Features Introduced

- Complete CPOE for All Clinical Services:
  - Laboratory orders with intelligent test selection
  - Radiology orders with appropriate imaging protocols
  - Nursing interventions and care directives
  - Dietary orders and nutritional management
  - All ancillary services coordination
- **Evidence-Based Order Sets:** Standardized protocols for common conditions and procedures
- **Clinical Pathway Integration:** Structured care guidance based on medical evidence
- **Advanced Decision Support:** Protocol-based recommendations with quality checkpoints

------

## Stage 5: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- Physicians:
  - Order all clinical services from single electronic interface with consistent workflow
  - Utilize evidence-based order sets ensuring comprehensive, standardized care for common conditions
  - Receive protocol-based recommendations for diagnosis-specific care pathways
  - Access integrated clinical guidelines and best practices during ordering process
- All Clinical Staff:
  - Follow standardized protocols with built-in quality checkpoints and safety measures
  - Access structured care plans with automated reminders for critical interventions
  - Track patient progress through established, evidence-based care pathways

------

## Stage 5: Why Standardization Improves Outcomes

- **Intent Achieved:** Evidence-based, standardized care delivery across entire organization
- Strategic Value:
  - Care consistency through proven protocols reduces practice variation
  - Intelligent order sets optimize resource utilization and clinical efficiency
  - Quality assurance built into workflow ensures adherence to best practices
  - Systematic outcome improvement through standardized, evidence-based care
- **Next Challenge:** Orders completed but no verification of actual care delivery - need closed-loop

------

## Stage 6: Advanced EMR Features - Closed-Loop Operations

- **Strategic Intent:** Complete Care Traceability with Closed-Loop Processes and Advanced Clinical Decision Support
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Ordering alone doesn't guarantee safe care delivery - need verification
  - Closed-loop ensures accountability from order entry through actual care
  - Real-time verification prevents errors at point of care delivery
  - Complete traceability enables rapid response to safety issues

------

## Stage 6: Closed-Loop Features Introduced

- Closed-Loop Medication Administration:
  - Barcode verification of patient, medication, dose, route, and timing
  - Real-time administration tracking with immediate documentation
  - Complete medication traceability from prescription to patient
- Advanced Clinical Decision Support:
  - Continuous patient monitoring with predictive alerting
  - Real-time safety monitoring across all clinical processes
  - Sophisticated analytics for proactive intervention
- **Comprehensive Quality Analytics:** Real-time dashboards and clinical metrics for continuous improvement

------

## Stage 6: What Clinicians Can Now Do

- Nurses:
  - Verify patient identity, medication, dose, and timing using barcode scanning before administration
  - Document medication administration with complete electronic traceability and audit trail
  - Receive real-time alerts for potential patient deterioration based on continuous monitoring
- Physicians:
  - Access sophisticated decision support based on complete patient data and clinical trends
  - Monitor patient status with predictive alerts for early intervention opportunities
  - Review comprehensive medication administration history with complete accountability
- Quality Managers:
  - Monitor hospital-wide safety metrics and clinical outcomes in real-time
  - Access complete audit trails for rapid incident investigation and resolution

------

## Stage 6: Why Closed-Loop Achieves Maximum Safety

- **Intent Achieved:** Complete care traceability with maximum patient safety
- Strategic Impact:
  - Unbreakable chain of accountability prevents errors and adverse events
  - Real-time verification ensures care delivery matches physician intent
  - Predictive capabilities enable proactive intervention before problems occur
  - Comprehensive quality monitoring drives continuous safety improvement
- **Next Challenge:** Individual patient care optimized - need population health management

------

## Stage 7: Ultimate EMR Features - Analytics and Intelligence

- **Strategic Intent:** Achieve Complete Paperless Operations with Advanced Analytics for Population Health Management and Continuous Quality Improvement
- Why These Features Make Sense:
  - Individual patient care optimized - now focus on population health outcomes

## Stage 7: Why Advanced Analytics Achieves Excellence

- Strategic Value:

## The Logic of Progressive EMR Development

- **Why Stage 1 First:** Must have electronic data before integration
- **Why Stage 2 Next:** Must integrate data before comprehensive documentation
- **Why Stage 3 Then:** Must document care before optimizing specific processes
- **Why Stage 4 Focus:** Must address highest-risk processes (medications) first
- **Why Stage 5 Expansion:** Must standardize all processes before optimization
- **Why Stage 6 Verification:** Must verify care delivery before population analysis
- **Why Stage 7 Analytics:** Must optimize individual care before population health

------

## Intent-Driven Implementation Strategy

- **Foundation Building (Stages 1-2):** Create reliable data infrastructure with basic intelligence
- **Care Documentation (Stage 3):** Enable comprehensive clinical recording and coordination
- **Safety Focus (Stage 4):** Address highest-risk clinical processes first
- **Standardization (Stage 5):** Implement evidence-based care across all services
- **Optimization (Stage 6):** Achieve maximum safety through verification and monitoring
